Automated Live Tissue Precision Slicer

A cutting-edge device developed to improve the slicing of organoids for research, integrating precision motor controls and real-time computer vision for enhanced accuracy and efficiency.

Developed by William Kraft, Beatriz Martinez-Martin, Isabella Lambros, and Yubing Sun at Life Science Laboratories, this device leverages precision motor controls and real-time computer vision to enhance the accuracy, efficiency, and repeatability of slicing, outperforming manual methods.

Device Specifications:

  • Hardware: Imaging tools, biological sample handling, and mechanical engineering.
  • Closest Commercial Analog: Vibratome.
  • Open Source License: MIT License.
  • Cost: Approximately $1500.
  • Source Repository: GitHub

Operation and Components:

  • Real-time Computer Vision: Dino-Lite digital microscope captures and processes images to detect organoid positions.
  • User Interface: Touchscreen for setting slicing parameters (thickness, speed, number of slices).
  • Software: Utilizes OpenCV for image processing without complex machine learning algorithms.

Validation Experiments:

  • Slice Thickness Accuracy: Measuring slices from agarose blocks to ensure they match target thicknesses (250 µm, 350 µm, 500 µm).
  • Slice Thickness Consistency: Evaluating consistency by calculating the coefficient of variation from multiple slices at 350 µm.
  • Organoid Viability: Assessing cell viability in sliced organoids using Live/Dead staining and comparing it to unsliced controls.

Dinosaur Game with Evolutionary Learning and Q-Learning

An implementation of the Dinosaur Game using Pygame, with AI agents trained using Q-learning and Evolutionary Learning techniques to avoid obstacles.

This project is a unique take on the classic Dinosaur Game, where the goal is to guide a dinosaur through a desert landscape, dodging obstacles like cacti. It showcases the implementation of two distinct artificial intelligence (AI) approaches for training the dinosaur to play the game effectively:

  • **Q-Learning:** A reinforcement learning technique where the AI learns by trial and error, associating actions with rewards. Over time, it figures out the best actions to take in different game situations to maximize its score.
  • **Evolutionary Learning:** A method inspired by natural selection, where the AI evolves a population of virtual dinosaurs. The fittest dinosaurs (those that survive the longest) pass on their traits to the next generation, gradually improving the overall performance of the population.

This project not only provides an engaging game experience but also serves as a valuable educational tool to understand and experiment with different AI learning paradigms. By observing the learning process and comparing the results of Q-learning and Evolutionary Learning, one can gain insights into the strengths and weaknesses of each approach.
